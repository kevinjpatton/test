About Me

Academic Journey & Philosophy

The Intersection of Thought and Play

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Philosophy at the University of Nebraska Omaha. My work sits at the intersection of traditional Epistemology and the emerging ethics of digital spaces.


I believe that philosophy shouldn't just be read—it should be experienced. This led me to my specialization in the Philosophy of Video Games, where I examine how virtual worlds challenge our concepts of agency, identity, and morality.

Core Focus Areas

  • Epistemology: Genus-species account of knowledge.
  • AI Ethics: Bias, digital personhood, and future governance.
  • Gaming: Ethics within interactive narratives.
